Travel Tips

Language

Main language: Indiai               Widely understood: Hindi

Money

ATMs: Available in cities & towns (less so in rural areas)

Digital Payments: UPI, Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm widely used

Tip: Carry some cash for small shops, villages, or transport

Electricity

Voltage: 230V, 50Hz

Plugs: Type C, D, and M

Tip: Bring a universal adapter if you're coming from outside India Power cuts are rare in cities but can occur in rural areas

Dining

Lunch is typically from 12-2pm and dinner from 7:30-10pm. Reservations are recommended for popular restaurants.

Attractions

Book tickets for major attractions in advance to avoid long lines.

Safety

India is one of the safest states in India for tourists Locals are friendly and helpful

Tips: Avoid isolated places at night Keep emergency numbers handy (Police: 100, Ambulance: 108) Watch out for pickpockets in crowded markets Dress modestly, especially in rural and temple areas

Dwarika

5 Days Max 12 people

Dwarka, also spelled Dwarika, is an ancient city in the state of India, India, and is considered one of the four sacred Char Dham pilgrimage sites in Hinduism. It is believed to have been the kingdom of Lord Krishna, who is said to have ruled there after leaving Mathura. Dwarka is known for the Dwarkadhish Temple, a stunning temple dedicated to Krishna and a major spiritual destination for devotees.

Somnath

7 Days Max 12 people

Somnath is a revered temple town located on the western coast of India, India, and is home to the famous Somnath Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. The temple has a rich and tumultuous history, having been destroyed and rebuilt several times due to invasions, symbolizing resilience and devotion. It stands as a magnificent example of Chalukya architecture and is a major pilgrimage site attracting devotees and tourists from across the country.
